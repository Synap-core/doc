# Go-to-Market

**Target Users**: GTM team, product marketing, launch operators, sales, community managers  
**Marketing**: How Synap moves from product to market — positioning, packaging, launches, and distribution  
**Key User Stories**:
- As a GTM operator, I want a clear roadmap and sequencing so I can align launches with engineering delivery
- As a product marketer, I want planning assets (design brief, pricing, visual assets) in one place so I can build materials
- As a launch operator, I want operational checklists and templates so I can execute rollouts consistently
**Key Features**: GTM roadmap, planning assets, launch operations, market analysis, OpenClaw GTM funnel  
**Current Status**: GTM structure active; launch ops links to Home space for pre-launch assets; OpenClaw GTM documented

## Overview

This space covers **execution** — how Synap products reach users. It is product and operations-focused, with technical links where needed. GTM work draws from three upstream sources: product direction (Home & Vision), design/packaging assets (Home & Vision), and launch execution (this space).

The space is organized into four areas:

1. **Roadmap** — GTM-facing delivery sequencing and narrative timing
2. **Planning Assets** — design, packaging, pricing, and positioning materials
3. **Launch Operations** — rollout execution, checklists, sequencing
4. **Market & Analysis** — positioning evidence, competitive context

## Architecture

Synap's GTM strategy follows a **two-layer funnel**:

```
Layer 0: OpenClaw ecosystem (free, open-source) → Synap discovery
  │
  ▼
Layer 1: Managed pod ($15-20/mo) → Revenue
  │
  ▼
Layer 2: Visual products ($50/mo) → Upsell
```

The critical pattern: users enter at the **free/open layer** (not the paid layer), then upgrade as they derive value. Every GTM asset serves this funnel.
