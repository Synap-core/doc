# Home & Vision

**Target Users**: All Synap team members — engineering, product, GTM, operations, design  
**Marketing**: The single source of truth for Synap's product direction, philosophy, and execution plan  
**Key User Stories**: 
- As a new team member, I want to understand Synap's vision, triptych model, and north star so I can align my work
- As a product leader, I want the roadmap and strategy documents accessible in one place so I can sequence releases

## Overview

This space is the **product vision and strategy hub** for Synap. It answers the questions: What are we building? Why? Where is it going? How do we get there? It serves as the north star that every feature, roadmap item, and GTM decision references.

The space is divided into two areas:

1. **Direction** — Vision, north star, strategy, product overview, roadmap, and related assets
2. **OpenClaw** — The OpenClaw integration strategy, current state, and research

## Architecture

Synap's product is organized around the **Triptych** — three composable concepts that form the entire product surface:

```
   Entities (Data)     Views (Lenses)      Channels (Interaction)
┌────────────┐    ┌────────────┐    ┌────────────┐
│ Typed,     │◄───│ Any view   │◄───│ AI + human │
│ related,   │    │ on the     │    │ conversation │
│ governed   │    │ graph      │    │            │
└────────────┘    └────────────┘    └────────────┘
```

Everything else — templates, the intelligence service, the browser app, documents — is infrastructure that enables or extends these three.

**The OS metaphor is load-bearing**: apps don't own data (they query entities), views are lenses not copies, AI is a privileged process that needs authorization, and the package manager (template engine) installs complete workspace configurations.
