# Messaging framework

## Master narrative

Synap is a sovereign AI second brain — the place where everything you encounter, think, read, save, or create goes, and where AI quietly turns that stream of raw input into structured, connected, queryable knowledge. You don't organize. You don't file. You just capture. Your data lives on infrastructure you own: a dedicated PostgreSQL database on your own server. Any AI model can connect. No lock-in. No loss. No organization required.

## Message hierarchy

### Level 1: Category and promise

- **Category:** Sovereign AI second brain / AI operating system for knowledge work
- **Promise:** Never lose anything. Never organize anything.

### Level 2: Strategic pillars

- **Pillar 1 — Capture anything, AI structures it:** Browser extension, Telegram bot, file drop, API hooks — everything enters Synap. AI automatically classifies entities, extracts properties, detects relationships, and builds the knowledge graph.
- **Pillar 2 — Your data, your infrastructure:** Your own PostgreSQL on your own server. Standard SQL, exportable anytime. If Synap disappears, your data and server keep running. Not shared infrastructure. Not someone else's cloud. Yours.
- **Pillar 3 — AI proposes, humans approve:** Every AI mutation goes through a reviewable proposal system. Full event chain audit trail. Human-in-the-loop governance by default. AI can be powerful without being dangerous.

### Level 3: Proof by surface

- **Browser (Electron desktop):** The primary interface — entity editor, view renderer, bento dashboard, channel conversations, sidebar navigation, 18 built-in apps, template-driven workspaces.
- **Relay (mobile):** Capture on the go, push notifications for proposals, entity browsing, channel responses, mobile-optimized bento dashboards.
- **Studio (web IDE):** Full-stack development environment — build custom views, compose new widgets, extend the platform with your own apps.
- **Intelligence Service:** Orchestrator agent + persona agents, 21 sub-routers, skill system, Hub Protocol, proposal governance, smart model routing (free/balanced/advanced/complex tiers).
- **Raycast extension:** Fast command-entry overlay for macOS — quick capture, quick lookup, deep-handoff to Browser.
- **Eve CLI:** Terminal-native Synap interface — entity CRUD, search, proposal review, pod management via text commands.

## Audience variants

- **Technical buyer (developer, self-hoster):** "Your own PostgreSQL. Open protocol. Self-host or managed. CI/CD pipeline already in place." Lead with architecture docs, Hub Protocol spec, Docker configs.
- **Operator/PM (project manager, team lead):** "AI governance for teams that can't afford mistakes. Every AI action is reviewable. Full audit trail. Your data, your rules." Lead with proposal system, entity profiles, workspace templates.
- **End user (personal knowledge worker):** "Never lose anything. Never organize anything. Capture a link, a note, a thought — AI handles the rest." Lead with emotional benefits (relief, trust) and the two promises.

## Do/Don't language

- **Do use:** "Sovereign," "your own server," "never lose/never organize," "AI proposes," "human-in-the-loop," "knowledge graph," "open protocol," "bring your own model," "structured memory," "event chain."
- **Avoid:** "Second brain as a service" (implies SaaS), "AI replaces you" (misleading — AI augments, doesn't replace), "no setup required" (self-hosted requires setup), "unlimited storage" (you manage the server, there are practical limits).
- **Never say:** "Cloud-hosted knowledge platform" — we are self-hosted-first, managed-second. That's the core trust argument.
