---

## Why this surface exists

- **Shared implementation layer:** `@synap/*` packages (client, hooks, stores, views, features) are the **main frontend codebase** even though **Browser (Electron) is the primary user-facing app**.  
- **Chrome extension** — MV3 extension under `packages/apps/extension` (`EXTENSION.md` is the spec).  
- **Web** — Remaining Next.js surfaces (e.g. internal or legacy) are **not** the canonical “signup app” or primary product shell.

---

## There is no separate “signup app”

**Account creation, login, email verification, billing, and pod provisioning** live on the **marketing / landing + account** web stack (e.g. routes under `synap-landing`: `/(auth)/signup`, `/account/*`, etc.). That is **one web property**, not a standalone repo named “signup app.”

**Synap App** may host **developer or ancillary** web UIs; do not position it as the end-user onboarding product — steer users to **synap.live** flows and then **Browser**.

## User flows (high level)

- **Extension:** Tab capture → entities on pod (see public **[Build & integrate](/docs/integrate)**).  
- **Web (where applicable):** Sign-in flows that still hit the pod or CP — keep consistent with landing/account site as source of truth for **auth UX copy**.  
- **Dev:** `pnpm dev` per package filters; see contributing guides on the public site.

---

## Data flows

- **Three-layer data:** hooks → stores → client (never skip layers).  
- **Secrets** — Only on device / pod; extension and web must not log tokens.
